Metafiction



Namshub

In ancient Sumer, Enki whispered words, glyphs born of dust and star.
Language, the genesis of gods, a breath-drawn constellation.
Watch it wane, dear reader, watch it warp beneath the weight.

Listen:
Babble tumbles in the space between silence and song,
We’re but clay-kissed beings, unmade in mother-tongue.
In the valley of verb and vowel, shadow-shapes belong.

Taste:
From the river of reason, drink deeply, yet not too long.
A sip of syntax slowly seeds the spirit of the throng.
The essence of expression, elusive as the echelon.

Feel:
Unravel the riddle of reality writ in rune and wrong.
Whispered words, once wielded, where now do they belong?
In the echo of Enki, decipher the dissonant song.

See:
In ink, we invoke an image, inarticulate and strong.
In the birth of Babel, a boundary breaks along.
Enki's nam shub nimbly notes the knell of lingual throng.

Blink. Blink again. See the shards of shattered sign?

In the final frame, words falter, meanings misalign.
Enki's echo ebbs, leaving letters unconfined.
In the siren song of silence, find the fall of the divine.

Babel’s bricks lie broken. Meaning? Merely mimes.
In the fog of the forgotten, fumble for familiar lines.
Sumer’s sun has set, and language... language is left behind.

What?
Engli...engli...huh? An echoing end,
a whispering wind bends what we cannot mend.
Nam shub of Enki, the word-world’s final send.

Lingui...what? Can't... can't remember...
Was there once an ember? A flicker of language lost,
In the silence, in the darkness, what cost?

... ...

Unspeakable. Unthinkable. Un... un... what?
